* Small entities are entitled to a 50% reduction, while micro entities are entitles to 75% reduction on most gov. fees.

Explanation of Small entities versus micro-entities: “Small entities” (a “person” who has NOT assigned and is under no obligation to transfer any rights to a third party OR owner is company with less than 500 employees which has not assigned, etc., any rights) are entitled to a 50% reduction of most government patent fees, “Micro entities” (Institutions of Higher Education OR “small entity” owners w/ no more than 4 previous applications, earn less < $150,162, and no assignments or the like to non-micro-entities) are entitled to a 75% reduction. See, 37 CFR 1.27 and 37 CFR 1.29, respectively for complete definitions. Also see 35 U.S.C. 41(h)(1) and 37 CFR 1.27(a).

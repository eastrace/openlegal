How it works

openlegal™ General Counsel is available in increments of 10 hours (openlegal GC), 20 hours (GC Plus) and 40 hours (GC Enterprise) per quarter (or 13-week period). Clients choose the amount of time to fit the size of their business. For companies with relatively little transactional work, 10-20 hours should be sufficient. For larger companies with routine contract, employemnt and IP work, 40 hours or more or may be in order. Additional packages of 10 hours may be added to any plan above.

Advantages for Businesses Without In-House Counsel

openlegal™ GC functions similar to an in-house legal team, but without the added overhead. This is the perfect solution for a business with routine legal needs, but without a large legal budget. Let openlegal™ GC handle your headaches so you can handle your business.

Advantages for In-House Counsel

openlegal™ GC is available to function as your safety-valve. For businesses that already have an in-house team or general counsel, openlegal™ GC is the perfect solution for variable workflow. Simply purchase the number of hours that fit your needs, and stop worrying about whether you should hire another associate. Then, when a surge in work occurs, simply pass it over to openlegal™ GC for processing. Before long, you will feel like our attorneys are yours--without the overhead, of course.

What Happens if More Time is Needed?

No worries. Simply contact us and we will work with you to cover the remainder of the quarter (or 13-week period). Additional packages of 10 hours may be added to any plan above.



Note: openlegal™ GC, GC Plus and GC Enterprise do not include services covered by other openlegal™ Packages, unless indicated above. Legal needs related to company formation, startup financing, intellectual property protection, and litigation are not typically included. Such services can be purchased separately through other openlegal™ Packages. openlegal™ GC, GC Plus, and GC Enterprise are designed to cover routine transactional needs typically handled by in-house general counsel or legal staff.
